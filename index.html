    Solar power forecasting to predict duck curve

    • Solar power is recognized as a key component of a green future, but excessive solar energy generation needs proper balancing to prevent damage to existing power sources.
    • Worked on the concept of the "duck curve," which represents the power required from non-solar sources during peak solar power generation.
    • Focuses on the calculation of the duck curve by predicting solar power generation using machine learning techniques.
    • Utilizes weather data parameters such as temperature, humidity, and wind temperature to predict solar output.
    • Enables efficient allocation of power demand to non-solar sources based on solar power generation trends.
    • Analyzes the typical pattern of solar power peaking during the daytime, followed by a sharp drop in non-solar demand and a steady increase as the sun sets.
    • Provides insights into adjusting non-solar sources for efficient power generation based on the duck curve.
    • Uses data from NSRDB to predict hourly Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) for VIT Chennai and corroborates findings with official sources.
    • Plots the duck curve for VIT Chennai and extends the model to observe GHI patterns in other locations across India.
